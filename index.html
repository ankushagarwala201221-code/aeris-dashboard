Environmental Visibility Factors

🌫 PM2.5 Pollution

PM2.5 particles are microscopic pollutants suspended in air.

High PM2.5 concentration reduces atmospheric visibility by scattering light and forming haze layers.

💧 Humidity

Humidity represents the amount of water vapor present in the atmosphere.

Humidity increases fog formation and atmospheric scattering.

🌧 Rain Detection

Rainfall affects visibility by introducing suspended droplets into the atmosphere.

Heavy rain causes optical obstruction and road hazards.

🌙 Ambient Light

Ambient light intensity is measured using the LDR sensor.

Low light conditions reduce visibility and increase danger.

Frequently Asked Questions

❓ What is VRI?

VRI estimates atmospheric visibility using pollution, humidity, rainfall and ambient light conditions.

❓ Why is PM2.5 dangerous?

PM2.5 particles reduce atmospheric clarity and affect respiratory health.

❓ How does humidity affect visibility?

Humidity creates microscopic droplets which increase fog formation.

❓ What happens during low VRI?

Low VRI indicates dangerous environmental visibility conditions.
