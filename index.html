Environmental Visibility Factors

🌫 PM2.5 Pollution

PM2.5 consists of microscopic particles suspended in the atmosphere. These particles originate from vehicles, industrial smoke, combustion and pollution.

High PM2.5 levels scatter light and create haze, reducing environmental visibility and increasing respiratory health risks.

💧 Humidity

Humidity measures the amount of water vapor present in air.

High humidity creates fog formation and increases atmospheric scattering, lowering long-distance visibility.

🌧 Rain Detection

Rain droplets obstruct the path of light, affecting visual clarity.

Heavy rainfall dramatically decreases visibility and increases transportation hazards.

🌙 Ambient Light

Environmental brightness is measured using the LDR sensor.

Low light conditions reduce visibility, especially when combined with rain or pollution.
