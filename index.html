Environmental Visibility Factors

🌫 PM2.5 Pollution

PM2.5 particles are microscopic pollutants suspended in air. These particles are produced from traffic emissions, industrial smoke, fossil fuel combustion and urban pollution.

High PM2.5 concentration reduces atmospheric visibility by scattering light and forming haze layers. Long-term exposure may also affect respiratory health and environmental quality.

💧 Humidity

Humidity represents the amount of water vapor present in the atmosphere.

As humidity increases, microscopic water droplets form in air, increasing atmospheric scattering and fog formation. This significantly lowers long-distance visibility during early mornings and rainy conditions.

🌧 Rain Detection

Rainfall affects visibility by introducing suspended droplets into the atmosphere.

Heavy rain causes light diffusion and optical obstruction, reducing road visibility and increasing transportation hazards. Combined rain and pollution conditions create severe atmospheric haze.

🌙 Ambient Light

Ambient light intensity is measured using the LDR sensor.

Low light conditions reduce visual perception and increase environmental darkness. Night conditions combined with fog or pollution create dangerous visibility environments.
