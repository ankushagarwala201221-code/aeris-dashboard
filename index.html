Environmental Visibility Factors

🌫 PM2.5 Pollution

PM2.5 consists of microscopic pollutants suspended within the atmosphere. These particles are generated through industrial emissions, fossil fuel combustion, traffic pollution and urban atmospheric contamination.

High PM2.5 concentration increases atmospheric haze, reduces visibility and impacts respiratory health significantly.

💧 Humidity

Humidity measures the amount of water vapor present within air. Increased humidity forms microscopic droplets which increase atmospheric scattering.

High humidity often creates fog formation and reduces long-distance visibility.

🌧 Rainfall

Rain introduces suspended droplets into the atmosphere, reducing visibility and increasing light diffusion.

Heavy rainfall combined with haze creates severe atmospheric visibility hazards.

🌙 Ambient Light

Ambient brightness is measured using the LDR sensor. Lower brightness levels reduce environmental clarity.

Night conditions combined with pollution or fog drastically reduce safe visibility.

Frequently Asked Questions

❓ What is VRI?

VRI stands for Visibility Risk Index. It predicts environmental visibility conditions using atmospheric sensor analytics.

❓ Why is PM2.5 harmful?

PM2.5 particles reduce atmospheric clarity and may affect respiratory health during prolonged exposure.

❓ How does humidity affect visibility?

Humidity increases microscopic water droplets in air which create fog and increase atmospheric scattering.

❓ What happens during low VRI?

Low VRI indicates dangerous visibility conditions and increased atmospheric haze.
